BubbleSort

Bubblesort works by repeatedly passing through the structure that holds the elements that are to be sorted. During a pass, the algorithm starts at one end and works its way through each position of the structure. At each position, it tests whether the current element and its (right) neighbor are correctly ordered. If that is not the case, the elements are swapped.

Note that after the first pass, the largest element will be at the very right - where it belongs. This is true because it was there from the beginning, or, because at each position the larger of two elements ended up on the right after comparison. This logic can be extended throughout the passes. I.e., after the second pass, the second largest element is correctly placed as the second to last element on the right. Then the third largest and so on, until everything is ordered.

This notion of letting the largest elements incrementally rise to their correct place to the right is what gives the algorithm its name. The largest elements "bubble up" through the array.
